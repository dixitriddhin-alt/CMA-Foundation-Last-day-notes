## 1️⃣ CONTRACT LAW

### Definition
**Contract:** An agreement enforceable by law between 2+ parties to do/not do something in exchange for consideration.

### Essential Elements (CADRES)
```
C - Consensus Ad Idem (mutual agreement)
A - Acceptance (clear & unconditional)
D - Definite Terms
R - Real & Free Consent
E - Eligible Parties
S - Supported by Consideration
```

### Formation of Contract
**Offer → Acceptance → Consideration → Executed**

| Stage | Definition | Key Point |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| **Offer** | Proposal with intention to be bound | Must be definite & communicated |
| **Acceptance** | Unqualified assent to terms | Must be identical to offer |
| **Consideration** | Value exchanged (money/goods/service) | Must move from both sides |
| **Executed** | Contract becomes binding | Parties obliged to perform |

### Memory Trick: "OFFER STACK"
- **O**pen the offer clearly
- **F**irmly accept (no conditions)
- **F**reely given (no coercion)
- **E**xchange of value
- **R**eal consent (no fraud/misrepresentation)

---

### Types of Contracts

| Type | Definition | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Express** | Terms stated clearly (oral/written) | "I'll sell you this car for ₹5 lakhs" |
| **Implied** | Terms inferred from conduct | Buying goods from shelf = implied contract |
| **Void** | Never enforceable from start | Contract with illegal object |
| **Voidable** | Valid but can be canceled by one party | Contract by minor (minor can cancel) |
| **Unenforceable** | Valid but cannot be enforced | Contract without required evidence |

---

### Discharge of Contract (How contracts END)

```
PERFORMANCE
    ↓
AGREEMENT (Both parties agree)
    ↓
BREACH (One party fails)
    ↓
FRUSTRATION (Impossible to perform - Act of God)
```

**Quick Definitions:**
- **Performance:** Parties fulfill all obligations
- **Breach:** One party fails to perform → Other party gets damages
- **Frustration:** Event beyond control makes performance impossible (no party liable)

---

### Remedies for Breach

| Remedy | Definition | Example |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Damages** | Monetary compensation | Seller pays ₹1 lakh to buyer |
| **Specific Performance** | Court orders party to perform | "You must deliver the goods" |
| **Injunction** | Court orders party NOT to do something | "Stop selling our product" |
| **Rescission** | Contract canceled + compensation | Buyer returns goods, seller refunds |

## 2️⃣ COMMERCIAL LAWS

### Sale of Goods Act

**Definition:** "A contract whereby seller transfers/agrees to transfer ownership for money consideration."

**Key Differences:**

| Aspect | Sale | Agreement to Sell |
|--------|------|-------------------|
| **Ownership** | Transfers immediately | Transfers in future |
| **Risk** | Buyer bears risk | Seller bears risk |
| **Conditions** | Become warranties | Remain conditions |

**Memory Trick: "SAG"**
- **S**aler transfers immediately (Sale)
- **A**greement transfers later
- **G**ood = physical good/commodity

---

### Cheque Law

**Definition:** "An instrument in writing drawn on banker for a fixed sum payable on demand."

**Types of Cheques:**

| Type | Features |
|------|----------|
| **Bearer Cheque** | Payable to anyone holding it (no signature needed) |
| **Order Cheque** | Payable to specific person (signature needed) |
| **Crossed Cheque** | Cannot be encashed at counter (must deposit) |
| **Post-Dated Cheque** | Drawn for future date |
| **Stale Cheque** | Not valid after 6 months |

---

### Bailment

**Definition:** "Delivery of goods by bailor to bailee for some purpose on condition that goods are returned."

**Duties of Bailee:**
1. Take reasonable care of goods
2. Use goods only for agreed purpose
3. Return goods on demand
4. Not mix goods with own goods

## 3️⃣ COMPANY LAW

### Types of Companies

| Type | Liability | Members | Shares | Minimum Members |
|------|-----------|---------|--------|-----------------|
| **Private Ltd** | Limited | Private | Non-transferable | 2 |
| **Public Ltd** | Limited | Public | Transferable | 7 |
| **Pvt Unlimited** | Unlimited | Private | Non-transferable | 2 |

---

### Memorandum of Association (MOA)

**Purpose:** Define company's objects & relationship with external world

| Point | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Content** | Name, address, objects, liability clause |
| **Who sees it?** | Public document |
| **Amendment** | Very difficult (requires special resolution) |

---

### Articles of Association (AOA)

**Purpose:** Define internal management & relationship between company & members

| Point | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Content** | Share transfer, voting rights, director powers |
| **Who sees it?** | Internal document |
| **Amendment** | Easier than MOA |

---

### Board of Directors

**Roles:**
- Day-to-day management
- Strategic planning
- Ensuring compliance
- Protecting stakeholder interests

**Powers Limited by:**
- MOA & AOA
- Companies Act
- General meetings resolutions

---

### Board Meetings

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Frequency** | At least 1 every 90 days |
| **Quorum** | 1/3 of directors (minimum 1) |
| **Notice** | 7 days advance |
| **Conducted** | In person or video conference |

## 4️⃣ BUSINESS COMMUNICATION

### Principles of Effective Communication

```
C - CLARITY (Clear & simple language)
O - OBJECTIVITY (Factual & unbiased)
M - MOREOVER (Additional relevant info)
M - MODESTY (Humble & respectful tone)
U - UNDERSTANDING (Consider audience)
N - NECESSARY (Only essential info)
I - IMMEDIATE (Timely delivery)
C - COURTESY (Polite & professional)
A - ACCURACY (Correct facts & figures)
T - TARGET-ORIENTED (Clear purpose)
E - EVIDENCE (Backed by facts)
```

---

### Types of Communication

| Type | Definition | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Formal** | Structured, official, documented | Emails, memos, reports |
| **Informal** | Casual, undocumented | Chat, casual conversation |
| **Verbal** | Spoken words | Meetings, presentations |
| **Non-verbal** | Body language, gestures | Eye contact, posture |
| **Written** | Documented words | Letters, reports, emails |

---

### Business Writing Tips

**Structure:**
1. **Opening:** Purpose clear in first line
2. **Body:** Support with facts/data
3. **Closing:** Clear action/next steps

**Tone:** Professional, concise, courteous

---

### Presentation Skills

| Aspect | Do ✓ | Don't ✗ |
|--------|------|--------|
| **Content** | Well-organized, relevant | Disorganized, off-topic |
| **Delivery** | Clear voice, eye contact | Monotone, looking at notes |
| **Visuals** | Simple, clear slides | Cluttered, too much text |
| **Time** | Within allocated time | Running over/under |

## 📝 Quick Reference Card

### Fastest Way to Answer Questions

**Q: Is this a contract?**
- Ask: Offer? Acceptance? Consideration? Free consent? → If all YES = Contract ✓

**Q: What happens on breach?**
→ Answer: Breach → Damages / Specific Performance / Injunction

**Q: Sale or Agreement to Sell?**
→ Answer: Ownership transferred now = Sale; transferred later = Agreement to Sell

**Q: What type of cheque?**
→ Answer: Crossed = Bank; Post-dated = Future; Bearer = Anyone
